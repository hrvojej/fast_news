Summary:

The Federal Emergency Management Agency (FEMA) has decided to cease enforcement of the Federal Flood Risk Management Standard, a rule designed to prevent flood damage to public buildings such as schools and fire stations. This decision, outlined in a February 4 memo by Adrian Sevier, FEMA's chief counsel, is considered a potential violation of federal law.

The Federal Flood Risk Management Standard, initially proposed in 2016 under President Barack Obama, mandates that public buildings damaged in a flood zone be rebuilt to withstand future floods to qualify for FEMA funding. President Donald Trump revoked the order in 2017, but President Joseph R. Biden Jr. reinstated it in 2021, with FEMA issuing a final rule last July.

However, with Donald Trump back in office, the order was again revoked, leading to FEMA's current pause in enforcement.

Experts, including David A. Super, a law professor at Yale University, and Jennifer Nou of University of Chicago Law School, argue that FEMA cannot simply stop enforcing a regulation without following a formal process. Chad Berginnis, executive director of the Association of State Floodplain Managers, stated that this action jeopardizing public safety and constitutes wasteful spending.

The home-building industry had previously opposed the rule, citing increased construction costs.

Interesting Facts:
  • ● The goal of the Federal Flood Risk Management Standard was not only to protect people and property but also to save taxpayers’ money.
  • ○ Former FEMA head, Deanne Criswell, said the rule was designed to put an end to "the cycle of response and recovery, and rinse and repeat".
  • ● Reversing a regulation like the Federal Flood Risk Management Standard can take months or even longer to complete legally.
  • ○ The decision by FEMA to pause the rule is in line with Donald Trump's view of expanding presidential authority.
  • ● Chad Berginnis gave the example of a water treatment plant, stating that without the rule, such facilities are more vulnerable, thus risking community safety.
